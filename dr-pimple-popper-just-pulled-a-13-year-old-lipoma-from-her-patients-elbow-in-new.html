Dr. Pimple Popper Just Pulled A 13-Year-Old Lipoma From Her Patient's Elbow In New Youtube Video

In her latest Youtube video, Dr. Pimple Popper is removing a massive lipoma and she shows every exciting moment of the procedure. FYI: "Lipomas are slow-growing, benign (non-cancerous) growths of fat cells," Dr. Pimple Popper shares with the video. "These cells are contained in a thin capsule that creates the appearance of a 'bump' right under the skin. Lipomas are typically not tender, move around easily, and dont require treatment."

This particular patient had other plans and preferred her lipoma treated and removed. Dr. Pimple Popper thinks it will be a straightforward extraction when she assesses the lipoma. Her patient has a bright pink arm band covering her elbow, but soon she won't need it.

Dr. Pimple Popper treats this unusual bump like any other. She starts with her scalpel and opens up space to squeeze and pull the lipoma out. The first step is easy, then comes the hard part. The dermatologist sticks her fingers in the opening to work around the lipoma and free it from its longtime home.

This lipoma is nice and cozy, so it takes her a lot of wiggling and trimming to get it moving. "I see it right there, I want to squeeze it out," Dr. Pimple Popper tells her patient. "I tend to have very strong fingers." She's confident she can get the lipoma out, but it's going to take some work. Her patient explains, "He's kinda attached to me."
