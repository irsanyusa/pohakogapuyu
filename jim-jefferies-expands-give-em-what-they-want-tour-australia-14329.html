Jim Jefferies Expands 'Give 'Em What They Want' Tour of Australia

Jim Jefferies will give ‘em more of what they want, as the Australian funnyman extends his homecoming tour.

Jefferies’ trek this August adds three new regional dates, swinging into Geelong, Newcastle and Canberra, according to Frontier Touring and More Talent, producers of the “Give ‘Em What They Want Tour.”

According to both companies, six shows on the previously-announced itinerary have already sold out, including a date at Melbourne’s Margaret Court Arena.

Tickets for the new shows will go on sale next Monday, June 3 from 11am local time via frontiertouring.com/jimjefferies.

Frontier Members get early access via the presale platform, which opens its virtual doors Friday, May 31 at 11am local time.

The Sydney-born, Perth-raised funnyman last toured these parts in 2022 for 27 sold-out shows across Australia and New Zealand.

With his freewheeling use of profanity (and a predilection for the C-word) and sharp political satire, Jefferies’ career blew up when his “gun control” sketch went viral on YouTube.

Today, he’s one of the most in-demand comedians on the planet, in a booming business for stand-up, playing the world’s biggest rooms, including Madison Square Garden and London’s The O2, and having them laugh in the aisles at Edinburgh Fringe, Melbourne International Comedy Festival, Reading and Leeds, and Glastonbury.  

It’s hard to miss the man on the small screen. He’s clocked up nine Netflix specials; helmed “The Jim Jefferies Show,” which ran for three seasons from 2017 to 2019; and fronts hit homegrown game show “The 1% Club” for free-to-air Channel 7.
